We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "**Comparative Genomics and Pan-genome Analysis of *Veillonella* Species Reveal a Moderately Open Pan-genome Structure**" for consideration for publication in [Journal Name].

*Veillonella* species are important members of the human microbiome, yet comprehensive comparative genomic analyses of this genus remain limited. Our study represents the first systematic pan-genome analysis of the *Veillonella* genus, providing novel insights into its genetic diversity and evolutionary dynamics.

### Key Findings

1. **Pan-genome Structure**: We identified a moderately open pan-genome (γ = 0.3386) comprising 4,456 gene families across 15 strains representing 6 species.

2. **Core Genome**: A conserved set of 1,125 core genes (25.2%) was identified, including essential metabolic pathways for lactate fermentation—a hallmark of *Veillonella* physiology.

3. **Phylogenetic Relationships**: Core gene-based phylogenetic analysis revealed clear species-level clustering, validating current taxonomic classifications.

4. **Clinical Relevance**: The identification of a substantial accessory genome (74.8%) has implications for understanding strain-specific adaptations and potential pathogenicity.

### Significance

This study provides a foundation for future research on:
- Probiotic applications of *Veillonella* in gut health and athletic performance
- Clinical management of *Veillonella* infections
- Microbiome engineering approaches

### Novelty

To our knowledge, this is the first comprehensive pan-genome analysis of the *Veillonella* genus. The findings advance our understanding of bacterial genome plasticity in host-associated bacteria and provide a valuable resource for the microbiome research community.
